Narwhals Research

Narwhals are medium-sized toothed whales that possess a large "tusk" from a protruding canine tooth. They live year-round in the Arctic waters around Greenland, Canada, and Russia.

Our Research

Our team conducts extensive research on narwhal migration patterns, communication methods, and population dynamics. We use state-of-the-art tracking technology to monitor their movements through Arctic waters.

Conservation Efforts

Narwhals face increasing threats from climate change and human activities. Our conservation efforts focus on protecting critical habitat areas and advocating for sustainable practices in Arctic regions.
